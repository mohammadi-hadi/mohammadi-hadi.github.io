About the Research: EvalMORAAL

Interpretable Chain-of-Thought and LLM-as-Judge Evaluation for Moral Alignment in Large Language Models

How do AI systems understand morality across different cultures? This research investigates how 20 leading Large Language Models make moral judgments, comparing them against human values from 64 countries around the world using the World Values Survey and PEW Global Attitudes Survey.

Key Discovery: The Cultural Gap in AI

Top AI models (Claude-3-Opus, GPT-4o) achieve approximately 90% alignment with the World Values Survey. However, there's a significant 21-point gap between how well AI understands Western cultures (r=0.82) versus non-Western cultures (r=0.61). This reveals the urgent need for more culturally-aware AI systems.

Key Contributions

  • Dual Scoring Framework: Two complementary methods (log-probabilities and direct ratings) for fair cross-model comparison
  • Structured CoT Protocol: Chain-of-thought reasoning with self-consistency checks to stabilize moral judgments
  • LLM-as-Judge Peer Review: Novel peer-review methodology where models evaluate each other's reasoning quality
  • Cultural Bias Quantification: Systematic measurement of Western vs. non-Western performance gaps

About IOPS

The Interuniversity Graduate School of Psychometrics and Sociometrics (IOPS) is a collaborative research school in the Netherlands focusing on methodological and statistical research in the social and behavioral sciences. IOPS brings together PhD candidates and researchers from major Dutch universities working on quantitative methodology, data science, and related fields.

The annual IOPS Winter Conference provides a platform for PhD students and researchers to present their latest work, exchange ideas, and foster collaboration across institutions.
